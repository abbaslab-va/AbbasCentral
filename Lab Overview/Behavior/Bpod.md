Developed by SanWorks, Bpod is the primary [[Software]] platform used in the Abbas Lab for designing and operating [[Behavior]] protocols.

These protocols take place in an operant behavior chamber, where ports can be programmed using the [[Bpod State Machine]] to respond to port entries by an animal, or to other state variables. These responses are either lighting/unlighting one or multiple ports, or dispensing water from the hypodermic tube situated inside the port.

For experiments with more complex [[Optogenetics]] protocols, the [[Pulse Pal]] is used in conjunction with the Bpod State Machine to precisely control timing of laser bursts and/or pulse trains. More simple laser behavior can be controlled directly from the state machine.

Lots of information on how to use Bpod is available on the wiki: https://sanworks.github.io/Bpod_Wiki/

Furthermore, the [[Abbas-WM]] repository contains Bpod scripts used in past [[Experiments]] as well as templates to design new state machines.
